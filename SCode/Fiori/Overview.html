SAP Fiori for iOS

Enterprise Design System

Overview

Our team has the opportunity to create a design system for mobile enterprise users–Fiori for iOS as part of the Apple+SAP partnership announced in 2016. Based on Apple’s Human Interface Guidelines and the award-winning design system—Fiori, Fiori for iOS aims to solve complex enterprise problems with consumer grade user experience.

Process

During the past three years, we have been enhancing the UI components in the SDK, creating guidelines and designing resources for product teams to consume.

I’m especially grateful for the opportunity to work with multiple groups of talented people across the organization. From interacting with internal product teams, to designing app flows with customers and partners, to consulting with designers and developers from Apple, all these experiences influence our decisions in defining a design system.

Design System

The structure of our design system is somewhat similar to the structure of atomic design, where small and independent building blocks can be combined into larger elements. With visual design and other platform specific topics as our foundation, we define components. Components are then combined into patterns that would handle different app scenarios.

As of 2019, our design system has a total of 39 components and 15 patterns. All of them has been implemented and are fully documented.

Key Deliverables

Deliverables of the partnership include a native SDK(Software Development Kit), Fiori for iOS design guidelines, a comprehensive Sketch UI library, and training events that enable partners and customers to easily build native iOS apps tailored to their business needs.

SAP Cloud Platform SDK for iOS

Supporting the SDK team has been one of our most important tasks. We work closely with developers from planing, ideation, design iteration to implementation. All of the UI components are designed to fit multiple use cases across different industries and adapt to various screen sizes. Our components also support dynamic type, to allow users to adjust their font size preferences.

Fiori for iOS Design Guidelines

Creating guidelines for our design system allow us to transfer concepts and knowledge to product teams. We aim to structure and organize our guidelines to align with the developer’s API doc, so that designers and developers can find related information more quickly. Our guidelines provide visual examples to convey best practices for each component.

Sketch UI Library (Stencils)

I am responsible for updating and maintaing our UI Library in Sketch, which we also called stencils. These files include all components in the design system, with sample screens and patterns, enabling product teams to produce high-fidelity designs efficiently. We have received a lot of positive feedback on how comprehensive the stencils are.

Native Applications

We have been collaborating with product stakeholders and designers from Apple at customer workshops. Following a three-day design sprint schedule, we are able to prioritize user needs and translate them into key screens using our design system.

Participating in customer design workshops has a great impact on our design decisions for the design system. This is an opportunity for us to test, validate and evaluate our components and decide if further enhancements are needed.

By using the design stencils, we are able to produce a high-fidelity prototype by the end of a three-day workshop. The SDK allows developers to quickly implement the designs without a lot of effort.

Training & Events

We organized training events to meet designers and developers in the Fiori for iOS community around the world, providing them with guidance and resources. We gathered feedback from these events to further improve our products.

I presented best practices about using our design system at a few events and even guided developers to design their very first app using our sketch stencils. These unique experiences have made me become a more confident designer.
